# Economic Record Transfer Package Audit

Date: 2026-06-01

## Judgment

Economic Record is a useful regular SSCI/JCR Q3 economics backup for the current paper, but it is not the requested single-journal 70-80% route.

The Economic Record transfer manuscript is intended only after APJFS rejection or after an explicit decision to move from the finance-journal framing to an applied-economics framing.

## Journal Fit

- Public JCR 2024 evidence lists Economic Record as an Economics journal in SSCI with Q3 status.
- Wiley Journal Finder search-result evidence gives a 37% visible acceptance-rate signal.
- The journal is a credible economics outlet with Australian relevance, making it a better regional backup than many generic or wrong-field Q3 journals.
- The manuscript fit is plausible if the paper is framed around market information, analyst attention, and future firm-level financial stress in Australia and Singapore.

## What Was Changed

- The transfer manuscript title now foregrounds market information and Australia-Singapore evidence.
- The abstract explicitly frames the paper as applied-economics evidence on market information and intermediary attention.
- The introduction now explains why an Australia-led two-market sample is relevant for an economics readership.
- The contribution paragraph now targets Australian and Asia-Pacific applied-economics work rather than only finance-journal audiences.
- The discussion emphasizes uneven market information environments rather than causal analyst effects.
- The data and results sections now separate the historical model panel from Chrome current-exchange exports and report an exchange-consistent ASX/SGX/Catalist robustness check.
- The transfer note prevents simultaneous submission with APJFS, AEL, Applied Economics, or APJAE.

## Evidence Strength

The empirical evidence remains the same as the APJFS and Applied Economics packages:

- APAC fixed-effect logit: analyst coverage odds ratio 0.568, p < 0.001.
- Exchange-consistent ASX/SGX/Catalist restriction: odds ratio 0.557, 18,411 observations, p < 0.001.
- Firm-clustered logit standard errors: coefficient -0.565, firm-clustered SE 0.078, p < 0.001.
- Stricter next-year stress label: odds ratio 0.713, p < 0.001.
- COVID outcome-year exclusion: odds ratio 0.580, p < 0.001.
- Current-stress control: odds ratio 0.749, p < 0.001.
- Onset-sample exclusion: odds ratio 0.785, p = 0.004.
- Analyst intensity: odds ratio 0.740, p < 0.001.
- Firm and year fixed-effects LPM: coefficient -0.030, firm-clustered SE 0.011, p = 0.006.

## Residual Risks

- Economic Record is not a 70-80% route; the current visible acceptance-rate signal is 37%.
- The paper remains associational, not causal.
- The stress label is broad and accounting-based, although the conservative controls, current-stress checks, onset checks, and firm fixed-effects LPM reduce the main reviewer risk.
- The paper uses Australia and Singapore only; do not claim comprehensive Asia-Pacific coverage.
- Chrome-controlled Capital IQ exports are provenance materials only; they should not be merged into the model until the 173 missing historical model-panel companies and FY2021 as-of-label gap are reconciled.
- Raw S&P Capital IQ exports and firm-level Capital IQ-derived panels must not be uploaded.

## Decision Rule

Use the Economic Record package only if:

1. APJFS is rejected without a fatal data objection; or
2. the user decides to prioritize a regular SSCI/JCR Q3 economics backup over the finance-journal fit.

Do not use the Economic Record package to claim that the exact single-journal 70-80% target has been achieved.
