# Regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 Journal Screen - Round 13

Date: 2026-06-01

## Purpose

This round checked whether SAGE business and management journals could provide a normal-reputation, scope-compatible route with a materially higher acceptance probability. The point was not to switch fields, but to verify whether a reputable adjacent publisher route could support the requested 70-80% expectation.

## Candidates and Evidence

### Business & Society

Decision: **Exclude as too selective and wrong contribution level.**

Evidence:

- A 2026 SAGE editorial reports that Business & Society had an acceptance rate of less than 5% for submitted manuscripts in 2024.
- The same editorial positions the journal as a high-impact business-and-society outlet, not a Q3 stability route.

Fit:

- The current paper is a finance/information-environment paper, not a business-and-society paper.
- Even if reframed around social or governance visibility, the official acceptance-rate evidence is far below 70-80%.

Result:

- Not a route for the current manuscript.

### Organizational Research Methods

Decision: **Exclude as too selective and wrong field.**

Evidence:

- SAGE editorial evidence reports that Organizational Research Methods has historically had a final acceptance rate around 15%.
- The journal is a highly selective management-methods outlet.

Fit:

- The paper uses standard logit and robustness designs; it does not make a methods contribution.

Result:

- Not a route for the current manuscript.

### Australian Journal of Management Revisited

Decision: **Still exclude as a probability route.**

Evidence:

- Round 10 already documented Australian Journal of Management as a regular SSCI/JCR Q3 management/business outlet with a SAGE official acceptance rate of 4.5%.

Fit:

- It is one of the few SAGE business/management examples with regional and finance/applied-economics plausibility, but the official acceptance rate is far below the requested target.

Result:

- Useful as a reputation benchmark, not as a 70-80% target.

## Interpretation

This SAGE sweep reinforces the same pattern found in Taylor & Francis and Emerald screens:

1. reputable business/management/finance-adjacent journals do not show 70-80% acceptance rates;
2. high acceptance-rate publisher pages are usually outside the paper's field;
3. acceptance-rate evidence is itself imperfect because calculation methods vary and author self-selection affects the number.

The current paper should therefore not be re-aimed at a SAGE business/management journal merely to chase a higher acceptance-rate number.

## Round 13 Conclusion

No SAGE business or management route improves on APJFS for the user's strict requirement. APJFS remains the strongest scope-reputation-Q3 primary route found, and the 70-80% expectation remains project-level rather than single-journal verified.
