# Regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 Journal Screen - Round 10

Date: 2026-06-01

## Purpose

This round checked additional normal-reputation finance, accounting, management, and nearby economics journals that could plausibly fit the paper without relying on CAS rankings. The screen focused on strict SCIE/SSCI or JCR evidence, official publisher pages where available, and whether any candidate could credibly support a 70-80% single-journal acceptance target.

## Candidates Checked

### Australian Journal of Management

Decision: **Exclude as a probability route; possible reputation backup only if the paper is reframed for management.**

Evidence:

- The public JCR 2024 export lists `AUSTRALIAN JOURNAL OF MANAGEMENT` in `MANAGEMENT` and `BUSINESS`, both SSCI and Q3.
- SAGE describes the journal as an international peer-reviewed journal publishing research in accounting, applied economics, finance, and other disciplines when applied to management.
- SAGE's current journal overview reports an official acceptance rate of 4.5%.
- SAGE submission guidelines state that there are no fees to submit or publish, with optional open access available separately.

Fit:

- Scope is defensible because the sample includes Australia and the journal accepts finance/applied-economics research.
- The paper would need a management-facing reframing around information environments and corporate distress.

Result:

- Not a 70-80% route. The official 4.5% acceptance rate is far below the user target and lower than APJFS/AEL-style routes.

### International Finance

Decision: **Possible finance backup, but not a high-probability route.**

Evidence:

- The public JCR 2024 export lists `International Finance` as `BUSINESS, FINANCE`, SSCI, Q3, and `ECONOMICS`, SSCI, Q2.
- Public journal-profile sources describe the journal as Wiley-published and focused on policy-relevant macroeconomics and finance, including financial markets and corporate finance.
- No current official high acceptance-rate signal was found.

Fit:

- Finance status is clean, but the journal is more international macro-finance and policy oriented than a firm-level analyst-coverage stress paper.
- The current APJFS manuscript would need substantial reframing toward cross-market information environments and international capital-market policy relevance.

Result:

- It can be kept as a later finance backup if APJFS rejects, but it cannot be represented as 70-80% stable acceptance.

### Accounting and Business Research

Decision: **Exclude unless the paper is rebuilt as an accounting-information paper.**

Evidence:

- The public JCR 2024 export lists `ACCOUNTING AND BUSINESS RESEARCH` as `BUSINESS, FINANCE`, SSCI, Q3.
- Taylor & Francis states that ABR publishes accounting papers and that the focus must be accounting rather than corporate finance or general management.
- Taylor & Francis states that non-open-access publication has no APC.

Fit:

- The current paper uses analyst coverage and financial stress, but the contribution is not primarily accounting.
- A serious ABR route would require much stronger accounting-information framing and likely additional accounting-specific theory.

Result:

- Not a stable 70-80% route and not a cleaner fit than APJFS.

### Journal of Post Keynesian Economics

Decision: **Exclude for this paper.**

Evidence:

- The public JCR 2024 export lists `JOURNAL OF POST KEYNESIAN ECONOMICS` as `ECONOMICS`, SSCI, Q3.
- Taylor & Francis reports an official 21% acceptance rate.

Fit:

- The scope is macro/heterodox economics. The current firm-level analyst-coverage and future-stress paper is not naturally a Post-Keynesian economics paper.

Result:

- Strict Q3 and official acceptance-rate evidence exist, but the acceptance rate is far below 70-80% and the scope fit is poor.

### Review of Pacific Basin Financial Markets and Policies

Decision: **Exclude under the strict user constraint.**

Evidence:

- The public JCR 2024 export lists the journal as `BUSINESS, FINANCE`, ESCI, Q4.
- The Pacific Basin topic fit is attractive, but the journal does not meet the requested regular SSCI/SCIE Q3 condition.

Result:

- Do not use as the main route unless the user explicitly relaxes the strict SSCI/SCIE Q3 requirement.

### Quarterly Journal of Finance

Decision: **Exclude under the strict user constraint.**

Evidence:

- The public JCR 2024 export lists `Quarterly Journal of Finance` as `BUSINESS, FINANCE`, ESCI, Q4.

Result:

- It is a finance journal, but it fails the requested regular SSCI/SCIE Q3 condition.

## Round 10 Conclusion

Round 10 did not identify a legitimate single-journal 70-80% route. The strongest new evidence is negative: Australian Journal of Management satisfies regular SSCI/JCR Q3 and has plausible finance/applied-economics scope, but the official SAGE acceptance rate is only 4.5%. Accounting and Business Research is also regular SSCI/JCR Q3, but its official scope is accounting-centered, not corporate-finance or general-management centered.

APJFS remains the best current primary target because it has the cleanest intersection of:

1. regular SSCI/JCR Q3 finance status;
2. Asia-Pacific capital-market scope;
3. acceptable normal-journal reputation;
4. current manuscript fit after the APJFS remap.

The 70-80% target remains defensible only as a project-level ladder probability, not as a verified single-journal acceptance probability.
