# Applied Economics Transfer Package Audit

Date checked: 2026-06-01

## Decision

Applied Economics is useful as the primary transfer manuscript after AEL, but it is not a strict Q3 substitute. The Taylor & Francis official page currently displays Applied Economics as Q2 best quartile with a 40% displayed acceptance rate. This makes it a credible probability-first SSCI-level backup, not the requested single-journal "SSCI/SCIE Q3 and 70-80%" solution.

## Package Role

The transfer package is intended for either of these cases:

1. AEL rejects the short letter for being too narrow, but not for a fatal data flaw.
2. The submission strategy moves directly to a fuller applied-economics article rather than a short letter.

The paper should not be submitted to AEL and Applied Economics at the same time.

## Current Manuscript Strengthening

The transfer manuscript has been upgraded from the older three-table draft to the current four-table robustness version. It now includes:

- 19,402 labelled Singapore-ASX firm-years and 12,303 future broad-stress events.
- Baseline market/year fixed-effect logit with analyst coverage odds ratio 0.568.
- Market split estimates for Singapore and ASX.
- Exchange-consistent ASX/SGX/Catalist restriction with odds ratio 0.557 and 18,411 observations.
- Conservative controls excluding direct or near-direct stress-label components.
- Firm-clustered logit standard-error robustness for repeated firm-year observations.
- Stricter next-year stress-label robustness requiring at least two next-year stress components.
- COVID outcome-year exclusion for fiscal-year 2020 and 2021 future-stress outcomes.
- Current-stress control and onset-sample exclusion.
- Analyst-intensity specification using log(1 + analysts).
- Firm and year fixed-effects linear probability model with firm-clustered standard errors.
- Conservative data availability language for Capital IQ licensing restrictions.
- Explicit boundary language that Chrome-controlled Capital IQ current-exchange exports are provenance materials only, not manuscript model inputs.
- Capital IQ analyst snapshot timing audit showing fiscal-year-end as-of dates and forecast-date timing safety.
