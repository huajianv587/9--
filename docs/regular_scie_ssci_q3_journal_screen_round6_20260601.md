# Regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 Journal Screen, Round 6

Date: 2026-06-01

## Purpose

This round checked broad open-access and business/economics journals that can look attractive because they have fast editorial metrics, broad scope, or third-party Q3/SSCI-looking profiles. The aim was to avoid replacing the APJFS route with a journal that is easier-looking but fails one of the user's strict conditions.

The user's conditions remain:

- regular SCIE/SSCI-level journal evidence, not CAS-zone logic;
- Q3 target unless a higher-quartile fallback is explicitly accepted;
- reputation not obviously below regular SSCI expectations;
- credible 70-80% acceptance likelihood;
- scope fit for an empirical listed-firm analyst-coverage and financial-stress paper.

## Result

Round 6 still does not identify a verified single-journal 70-80% regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 route. The additional journals either have too-low official acceptance rates, ESCI rather than SSCI/SCIE in publisher-facing evidence, wrong scope, or quartile mismatch.

## Candidate Checks

| Journal | Official or publisher-facing signal checked | Acceptance signal | Decision |
| --- | --- | ---: | --- |
| Cogent Economics & Finance | Taylor & Francis page reports broad economics/finance scope, Q1 Impact Factor best quartile, 17% acceptance rate, and Web of Science ESCI indexing | 17% | Exclude for strict target. It is broad and finance-relevant, but it is not Q3 and publisher-facing indexing is ESCI, not SSCI/SCIE. It also has a low official acceptance rate. |
| Cogent Business & Management | Taylor & Francis page reports Q2 Impact Factor best quartile and 20% acceptance rate | 20% | Exclude for strict target. Broad business scope does not override Q2 status, open-access positioning, and low official acceptance. |
| Transnational Corporations Review | Taylor & Francis page reports ESCI/Scopus indexing, transnational corporation/FDI scope, and 32% acceptance rate; third-party public pages may show business/economics quartiles | 32% | Exclude for strict target. The publisher-facing indexing is ESCI and the scope is multinational corporations/FDI rather than analyst coverage and financial stress. |
| Transformations in Business & Economics | Public Web of Science/JCR-style mirrors show SSCI and business/economics Q3-type signals; the journal's historical editorial states acceptance was around 10-12% at that time | no current official high acceptance found; historical editorial says 10-12% | Not a 70-80% route. It can be kept only as a broad business/economics name to re-check institutionally, but it is not stronger than APJFS and does not solve the high-probability requirement. |

## Practical Implication

Do not replace APJFS with a broad OA journal or ESCI journal to chase perceived ease. The manuscript's current best reputation-preserving route remains APJFS. If strict Q3 must be retained, the next useful work is not a Cogent-style remap; it is either:

1. institutionally verify a truly scope-fitting SSCI/SCIE Q3 journal with an official acceptance signal above 50%; or
2. accept that 70-80% can only be a sequential portfolio goal, not a verified single-outlet property.
