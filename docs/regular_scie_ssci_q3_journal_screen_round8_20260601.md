# Regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 Journal Screen Round 8

Date checked: 2026-06-01

## Purpose

This round checks Emerald journals because Emerald's current journal pages often display official acceptance-rate metrics and indexing lists. These are useful for the user's probability requirement because they are publisher-facing rather than third-party rumors.

## Candidate Checks

| Journal | Current official/public evidence checked | Acceptance signal | Decision |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Managerial Finance | Emerald page lists economics/finance indexing but publisher-facing Web of Science status is Emerging Sources Citation Index rather than SSCI/SCIE. | 16.3% official Emerald rate | Exclude for strict target. It is finance-relevant but not strict SSCI/SCIE on the publisher-facing indexing list, and the official acceptance rate is far below 70-80%. |
| Journal of Financial Economic Policy | Emerald page describes a strong finance/economics policy scope, including capital markets, corporate finance, governance, behavioral economics and finance, and real estate finance. | 13.5% official Emerald rate | Exclude as a high-probability route. Scope is plausible, but the acceptance rate is far below target and this does not improve on APJFS for the user's strict route. |
| Journal of Financial Crime | Emerald page lists economics/finance directories and Scopus, but the scope is financial crime rather than analyst coverage and future financial stress. | 18.5% official Emerald rate | Exclude. It is wrong-scope for the manuscript and far below 70-80%. |
| Multinational Business Review | Emerald page lists SSCI, Scopus, and 2024 Impact Factor 2.7. Scope is international business and multinational enterprise research. | 11.9% official Emerald rate | Exclude. It is reputable and SSCI-facing, but both scope and acceptance probability are poor for this paper. |
| Baltic Journal of Management | Emerald page lists Journal Citation Reports and Social Sciences Citation Index. Scope is management rather than finance/economics. | 5.7% official Emerald rate | Exclude. It is a strict-index example showing that regular SSCI business/management journals are much more selective, not a 70-80% route. |

## Conclusion

The Emerald official-metrics sweep is strong evidence against the existence of a reputable, scope-fitting, regular SSCI finance/business outlet with 70-80% acceptance. Even where indexing is stronger, official acceptance rates are low. Where scope is closer to finance, publisher-facing index status is often ESCI or the acceptance rate remains far below target.

No journal in this round replaces APJFS as the best regular Q3 finance route.
