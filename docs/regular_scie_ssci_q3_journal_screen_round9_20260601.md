# Regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 Journal Screen Round 9

Date checked: 2026-06-01

## Purpose

This round checks cross-publisher and regional economics/business candidates that were not fully covered by the Taylor & Francis and Emerald sweeps. The aim is to test whether a regular SSCI/JCR Q3 outlet with better geographic or business/economics fit can get closer to the user's 70-80% acceptance requirement.

## Candidate Checks

| Journal | Current official/public evidence checked | Acceptance signal | Decision |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Singapore Economic Review | Public JCR export lists `SINGAPORE ECONOMIC REVIEW` as ECONOMICS, SSCI, JIF 1.2, Q3. NTU's page describes it as a premier quarterly journal publishing policy, applied economics, and theoretical studies, with emphasis on Asian economies and financial/social challenges. | No official high acceptance-rate signal found | Possible economics backup for geographic fit, but not a verified 70-80% route. It is economics rather than finance, and the paper would need a stronger applied-economics/Asia framing. |
| Academia Revista Latinoamericana de Administracion | Public JCR export lists it as MANAGEMENT, SSCI, Q3 and BUSINESS, SSCI, Q3. Emerald page reports SSCI ranking, double-anonymous review, and scope including finance, accounting, capital markets, and governance. | 18.7% official Emerald rate | Exclude as high-probability route. It is a real SSCI/Q3 business-management journal with some finance scope, but it is Latin America oriented and far below 70-80%. |
| Global Economic Review | Taylor & Francis page reports Social Sciences Citation Index indexing, Asia/East Asia economics scope, and official 6% acceptance. Its own metrics show Q1 Impact Factor best quartile, while third-party and public JCR-style sources may show economics Q3 in some views. | 6% official Taylor & Francis rate | Exclude. Scope is partly Asia/economics relevant, but the official acceptance rate is extremely low and it is not a stable Q3/high-probability outlet. |
| German Economic Review | Public JCR export lists German Economic Review as ECONOMICS, SSCI, JIF 1.4, Q3. De Gruyter instructions provide submission guidance, but no official high acceptance-rate signal was found. | No official high acceptance-rate signal found | Exclude as probability route. It is a regular economics journal but the paper's Singapore-ASX analyst-coverage finance framing is not a natural match, and there is no 70-80% evidence. |
| Amfiteatru Economic | Public JCR export lists it as ECONOMICS, SSCI, Q2 and MANAGEMENT/BUSINESS, SSCI, Q3. The journal's own metrics page shows 2024 JCR indicators and Q2 economics status; public sources did not produce a credible official acceptance rate. | No official high acceptance-rate signal found | Exclude as exact route. It is not a clean finance/economics Q3 probability outlet for this paper, and no 70-80% acceptance evidence was found. |

## Conclusion

Round 9 adds one useful possible backup, Singapore Economic Review, because it is SSCI Q3 economics and has Asia/Singapore relevance. However, it still does not meet the user's exact target because no official or credible 70-80% acceptance evidence was found.

The strongest verified probability facts remain negative:

- ARLA: 18.7% official Emerald acceptance.
- Global Economic Review: 6% official Taylor & Francis acceptance.
- Other regional/economics Q3 candidates: no official high-acceptance evidence found.

Therefore APJFS remains the best regular-Q3 finance fit, while Singapore Economic Review can be noted only as a possible economics backup if the paper is reframed more explicitly around applied economics and Asia-Pacific information environments.
