# Regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 Journal Screen Round 7

Date checked: 2026-06-01

## Purpose

This round checks additional Taylor & Francis and publisher-facing candidates that can look plausible because they are broad economics, finance, or business journals with visible journal metrics. The goal is to find, or rule out, a current official-source journal that simultaneously satisfies:

- regular SCIE/SSCI-level indexing, not merely ESCI/Scopus;
- JCR Q3 or a defensible Q3 equivalent under journal-facing evidence;
- scope fit for an empirical listed-firm analyst-coverage and financial-stress paper;
- official or otherwise credible acceptance evidence close to 70-80%;
- reputation not obviously below a regular SSCI finance/economics journal.

## Candidate Checks

| Journal | Current official/public evidence checked | Acceptance signal | Decision |
|---|---|---:|---|
| International Economic Journal | Taylor & Francis page describes broad international economics scope and lists ESCI, EconLit, IBSS, and Scopus indexing. | 8% on Taylor & Francis | Exclude. It is not publisher-facing SSCI/SCIE and the acceptance rate is far below 70-80%. The paper also lacks a strong international-economics framing. |
| Economic Research-Ekonomska Istraživanja | Taylor & Francis page shows broad economics scope and only Scopus-style citation metrics in the current visible metrics block. | 22% on Taylor & Francis | Exclude. The acceptance rate is far below target and the current visible metrics do not establish a strict SSCI/SCIE Q3 route. |
| Quantitative Finance | Taylor & Francis page shows quantitative-finance scope, Q2 Impact Factor best quartile, and rigorous peer review. | 23% on Taylor & Francis | Exclude. It is more selective than the target, not Q3, and the current paper is an empirical corporate-panel finance paper rather than a quantitative-finance methods paper. |
| Representation | Taylor & Francis page shows a 40% acceptance rate and political-representation/election scope. | 40% on Taylor & Francis | Exclude. Even the acceptance rate is below 70-80%, and the scope is political science, not finance/economics/business. |
| Research in Hospitality Management | Rechecked because it has the official high acceptance-rate signal found earlier. Taylor & Francis page is hospitality/tourism oriented and not the paper's field; publisher-facing indexing is not strict SSCI/SCIE for the target. | 70% on Taylor & Francis | Exclude. It remains the only official near-target acceptance-rate signal found, but it fails the strict index/scope/reputation fit for this finance paper. |

## Conclusion

Round 7 does not change the route. The official high-acceptance evidence continues to appear only in wrong-field or non-strict-index journals. Broad economics/finance journals with better scope fit show official acceptance rates between 8% and 23% in this round.

The most defensible route remains:

1. APJFS as the strongest regular SSCI/JCR Q3 finance fit.
2. APJAE as a secondary SSCI accounting/economics/finance transfer candidate, but not high probability.
3. AEL or Applied Economics as probability-first fallback only, not strict Q3/70-80 proof.

The exact single-journal requirement remains unresolved: no official-source regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 finance/economics/business journal with a credible 70-80% acceptance signal has been verified.
