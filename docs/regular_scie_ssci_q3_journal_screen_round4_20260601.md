# Regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 Journal Screen, Round 4

Date: 2026-06-01

## Purpose

This round specifically searched for official, publisher-displayed acceptance-rate evidence in regular journals that could plausibly fit a finance, economics, accounting, or business version of the manuscript. The screen remains JCR/SCIE/SSCI based and does not use CAS or CAS-zone classifications.

## Result

Round 4 still does not identify a reputable regular SCIE/SSCI/JCR Q3 single-journal target with a verified 70-80% acceptance rate for the current paper.

The strongest evidence works in the opposite direction: when a finance/economics/business journal is clearly regular and indexed at the expected level, the official acceptance rates found are far below 70-80%. When a 70% acceptance rate is publicly displayed, the journal found in this sweep is not a SCIE/SSCI finance/economics target and is not usable for this paper.

## Manuscript Implication

The right way to move the paper toward the user's probability objective is not to chase a single claimed 70-80% regular SSCI journal without evidence. The defensible path is:

1. Keep APJFS as the regular-Q3 finance primary route.
2. Strengthen the paper's reviewer-risk profile for regular SSCI review.
3. Maintain APJAE and AEL/Applied Economics as transfer routes.
4. Treat project-level 70-80% as a portfolio goal across sequential submissions, not as a verified single-journal acceptance rate.

## New Paper Strengthening Added After This Screen

Because the journal screen did not produce a credible 70-80% single outlet, the manuscript was strengthened with a firm and year fixed-effects linear probability robustness check. This check uses within-firm changes in analyst coverage and firm-clustered standard errors. The analyst-coverage coefficient remains negative and statistically significant, which directly addresses the likely reviewer concern that the pooled result is only a cross-sectional comparison between visible and less visible firms.
