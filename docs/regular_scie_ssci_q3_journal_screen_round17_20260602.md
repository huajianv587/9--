# Regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 Journal Screen Round 17

Date: 2026-06-02

## Purpose

This round refreshes the journal-route evidence after the Chrome Entity-ID repeatability pilot. The user's constraint is unchanged:

- regular SSCI/SCIE-level outlet;
- JCR Q3 where possible, without relying on CAS ranking;
- normal scholarly reputation;
- scope fit for an empirical analyst-coverage / financial-stress paper;
- credible single-journal 70-80% acceptance evidence.

## Current Source Recheck

### APJFS Primary Route

The Korean Securities Association describes Asia-Pacific Journal of Financial Studies as its official journal, selected for SSCI coverage since 2006, publishing bimonthly, and welcoming finance papers through Wiley. The public JCR 2024 export records `Asia-Pacific Journal of Financial Studies` as `BUSINESS, FINANCE`, `SSCI`, `JIF 1.5`, `Q3`.

Decision: APJFS remains the best regular finance-journal fit for the current manuscript, but this recheck still did not find an official single-journal 70-80% acceptance signal.

### Economic Record Transfer Route

The public JCR 2024 export records `ECONOMIC RECORD` as `ECONOMICS`, `SSCI`, `JIF 1.2`, `Q3`. The package retains Economic Record as the strongest regular economics transfer route when the framing is broadened from finance-market microstructure to applied financial economics.

Decision: Economic Record remains a defensible regular SSCI/JCR Q3 transfer route, but the visible acceptance-rate signal in the existing package is far below 70-80%, and this round did not find better official evidence.

### Applied Economics / Applied Economics Letters

The public JCR 2024 export records:

| Outlet | JCR 2024 signal | Current publisher acceptance signal | Route implication |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Applied Economics | `ECONOMICS`, `SSCI`, `JIF 2.1`, `Q2` | 40% Taylor & Francis signal | Broader transfer route, not strict Q3 and not 70-80. |
| Applied Economics Letters | `ECONOMICS`, `SSCI`, `JIF 1.3`, `Q3` | 35% Taylor & Francis signal | Good compressed empirical-letter fallback, not 70-80. |

Taylor & Francis defines the displayed acceptance rate as accepted articles as a percentage of papers receiving a final decision, with usage and acceptance-rate data updated annually in February. Wiley's metrics overview similarly defines acceptance rate by final-decision period and warns that scope, peer-review model, commissioning strategy, document type, and study design can affect the metric.

Decision: AEL remains the most mechanically compatible strict Q3 fallback among the Taylor & Francis economics options, but the official acceptance signal is 35%, not 70-80.

### Other Previously Screened Adjacent Outlets

- Asia-Pacific Journal of Accounting & Economics remains scope-adjacent and appears as SSCI Q3 in Business, Finance in the public JCR 2024 export, but its economics category is Q2 and it does not solve the 70-80 acceptance constraint.
- International Finance appears as SSCI Q3 in Business, Finance and Q2 in Economics in the public JCR 2024 export, but its scope is less direct for a firm-level analyst-coverage/stress paper, and no 70-80 acceptance evidence was found.
- Secondary high-acceptance claims, especially agency/blog-style or CAS-ranking claims, remain excluded because they are not publisher-official, not manuscript-specific, and conflict with the user's "not CAS" requirement.

## Decision

Round 17 does not change the route:

1. APJFS first submission remains the best regular SSCI/JCR Q3 finance fit.
2. Economic Record remains the strongest regular SSCI/JCR Q3 economics transfer route.
3. Applied Economics / AEL remain prepared fallbacks, with AEL the strict Q3 compressed-letter option.

The requested exact condition, "regular SSCI-level Q3 and stable 70-80% single-journal acceptance", remains unproved. The defensible operating strategy is still a project-level ladder, not a guaranteed single-journal route.
