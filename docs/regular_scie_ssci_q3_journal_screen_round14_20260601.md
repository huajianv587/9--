# Regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 Journal Screen - Round 14

Date: 2026-06-01

## Purpose

This round checked whether direct finance journals from Elsevier or Springer could offer a better probability route than APJFS while keeping normal SSCI-level reputation and scope fit.

## Candidates Checked

### Finance Research Letters

Decision: **Not the requested Q3 route; possible higher-tier transfer only if the user relaxes Q3.**

Evidence:

- Elsevier's ScienceDirect journal insights page lists Finance Research Letters as SSCI-indexed.
- The same page reports a 2024 Impact Factor of 6.9, a CiteScore of 10.7, and an official acceptance rate of 35%.
- The publisher page states that the journal invites submissions in all areas of finance and aims for rapid publication of important new results.
- The subscription route states that no publication fee is charged to authors; open access is optional with an APC.

Fit:

- Scope fit is strong because the paper is a finance/information-environment paper.
- However, the journal is a high-impact finance letters outlet rather than a Q3 stability route.

Result:

- It is not compatible with the user's strict "Q3 and 70-80%" requirement. It can be kept only as a higher-tier stretch/transfer option, not as the primary route.

### Financial Innovation

Decision: **Exclude as primary route.**

Evidence:

- Springer states that Financial Innovation is indexed in SSCI and covers broad financial innovation, financial technology, financial markets, financial risk, corporate finance, big data analytics in finance, AI, blockchain, and related areas.
- The journal is an open-access Springer journal sponsored by Southwestern University of Finance and Economics.

Fit:

- The current paper is about analyst coverage and future financial stress, not financial innovation, fintech, blockchain, GenAI, or AI-enabled finance.
- The paper would need a substantially different framing and probably new technology/innovation content.

Result:

- Not a clean scope fit and not a Q3/70-80 route.

### Asia-Pacific Financial Markets

Decision: **Possible scope-adjacent backup only if index/quartile is separately verified; not a replacement for APJFS.**

Evidence:

- Springer describes Asia-Pacific Financial Markets as a hybrid journal with a subscription publishing model and optional open access.
- The journal's issue history and special-issue topics indicate Asia-Pacific financial-markets scope.
- The current public publisher page reviewed here did not provide an official high acceptance-rate signal.

Fit:

- Regional and financial-market scope is plausible.
- However, without current strict SSCI/JCR Q3 evidence and without an official high acceptance-rate signal, it cannot replace APJFS.

Result:

- Keep only as a possible later backup for manual verification, not as a current target.

## Round 14 Conclusion

The direct finance-journal route does not solve the user's exact target. Finance Research Letters is a strong-scope journal with an official 35% acceptance rate and no mandatory publication fee under the subscription route, but it is not Q3 and not 70-80%. Financial Innovation and Asia-Pacific Financial Markets do not replace APJFS under the strict route.

APJFS therefore remains the best current primary match for:

- regular SSCI/JCR Q3 finance status;
- Asia-Pacific capital-market scope;
- normal scholarly reputation;
- current manuscript fit after the APJFS remap.
