# Regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 Journal Screen - Round 12

Date: 2026-06-01

## Purpose

This round searched specifically for official publisher pages with visibly high acceptance rates. The aim was to test whether a 50-70%+ acceptance signal could be combined with the user's substantive constraints: regular SCIE/SSCI-level reputation, Q3-like level, and plausible scope for an empirical analyst-coverage and financial-stress paper.

## High-Acceptance Signals Found

### Forest Science and Technology

Decision: **Exclude as wrong-field.**

Evidence:

- Taylor & Francis reports a 60% acceptance rate.
- The journal has a 2024 Impact Factor and Q2 Impact Factor Best Quartile on the publisher page.
- The scope is forest science, silviculture, forest protection, forest engineering, forest management, forest economics, information and policy sciences, and forest recreation.

Fit:

- The paper has no forestry, natural-resource, or environmental-management research question.

Result:

- Official high acceptance exists, but it is not a legitimate route for this manuscript.

### Journal of Wine Research

Decision: **Exclude as wrong-field and not strict SCIE/SSCI finance/economics.**

Evidence:

- Taylor & Francis reports a 50% acceptance rate.
- The scope is viticulture, oenology, wine trade, wine tasting, and interdisciplinary wine-related research.

Fit:

- The paper has no wine, viticulture, oenology, or wine-trade component.

Result:

- Official high acceptance exists, but it is not a legitimate route for this manuscript.

### Asian Journal of Sport History & Culture

Decision: **Exclude as wrong-field.**

Evidence:

- Taylor & Francis reports a 55% acceptance rate.
- The scope is sport history and culture.

Fit:

- No substantive relationship to corporate finance, analyst coverage, or financial stress.

Result:

- Not a legitimate route.

### The Italianist

Decision: **Exclude as wrong-field.**

Evidence:

- Taylor & Francis reports a 70% acceptance rate.
- The journal is an Italian studies outlet covering literature, culture, history, art history, cinema, linguistics, and related fields.

Fit:

- No substantive relationship to the current paper.

Result:

- This is the clearest official 70% signal in the sweep, but it is completely outside the manuscript's field.

### Substance Use & Misuse

Decision: **Exclude as wrong-field.**

Evidence:

- Taylor & Francis reports a 45% acceptance rate.
- The scope is substance use, misuse, addiction, nicotine, alcohol, eating disorders, and related public-health/social-pharmacology topics.

Fit:

- No substantive relationship to the current paper.

Result:

- It confirms that higher official acceptance rates exist, but not in a scope-compatible finance/economics route.

### Journal of Applied Statistics: Environmental Statistics and Data Science

Decision: **Exclude as probability route.**

Evidence:

- Taylor & Francis reports a 13% acceptance rate.
- The outlet is statistics/data-science oriented rather than finance-journal oriented.

Fit:

- Methodological overlap exists only at a generic statistical level; the paper's contribution is not environmental statistics or data science.

Result:

- Not a high-probability route and not a natural target for the current manuscript.

## Round 12 Conclusion

The high-acceptance official signals found in this sweep do not solve the user's target. The journals with 50-70% acceptance are outside the paper's field, while finance/economics/business outlets with normal SSCI-level reputation remain much lower in visible acceptance-rate evidence.

The practical implication is unchanged:

- Do not force the paper into a wrong-field high-acceptance journal.
- Keep APJFS as the best scope-reputation-Q3 match.
- Treat any 70-80% expectation as a project-level ladder target unless a scope-compatible finance/economics/business journal publishes credible current high-acceptance evidence.
