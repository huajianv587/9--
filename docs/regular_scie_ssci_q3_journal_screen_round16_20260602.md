### Acceptance-Rate Evidence

This round did not find a credible official single-journal 70-80% acceptance signal for a normal-reputation, scope-fitting SSCI/SCIE Q3 finance/economics outlet.

Publisher metric definitions matter:

- Wiley explains acceptance rate as the percentage of papers accepted among papers reaching final decision, and notes that scope, peer-review model, commissioning strategy, document type, and study design can affect the metric.
- Taylor & Francis official journal pages define acceptance rate as accepted articles as a percentage of papers receiving final decision, with data updated annually in February where available.

Current usable outlet signals remain below the requested 70-80% threshold:

| Outlet | Current acceptance signal used in package | Strict 70-80 result |
|---|---:|---|
| Applied Economics Letters | 35% official Taylor & Francis signal | Fails 70-80 |
| Applied Economics | 40% official Taylor & Francis signal, but Q2 best quartile | Fails strict Q3/70-80 |
| Economic Record | 37% visible Wiley-side signal recorded in the transfer package | Fails 70-80 |
| APJFS | no credible official 70-80 signal found | Cannot claim 70-80 |

### Rejected High-Acceptance Claims

Search results surfaced secondary Chinese-language or promotional claims suggesting near-80% acceptance for outlets such as Journal of Business Economics and Management or Applied Economics Letters. These claims are not accepted as proof for this project because:

1. they are not publisher-official or JCR/Clarivate evidence;
2. they often mix CAS ranking, informal agency promotion, or reputation-shortcut language, contrary to the user's "not CAS" requirement;
3. they conflict with publisher-visible acceptance-rate signals already recorded for similar Taylor & Francis economics outlets;
4. they do not prove manuscript-specific fit or editorial probability.

## Decision

Round 16 does not change the target ladder.

The honest current route remains:

1. APJFS first: regular SSCI/JCR Q3 finance route, best scope fit, but not verified 70-80.
2. Economic Record transfer: regular SSCI/JCR Q3 economics backup, visible 37% signal, not 70-80.
3. Applied Economics / AEL: transfer/fallback routes with visible acceptance signals well below 70-80.

The requested exact condition, "regular SSCI-level Q3 and stable 70-80% single-journal acceptance", remains unproved.
