# Regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 Journal Screen - Round 15

Date: 2026-06-01

## Purpose

This round checked whether reputable economics and finance-adjacent journals from Wiley and Elsevier could improve the submission ladder while preserving the user's constraints:

- regular SCIE/SSCI-level status, not CAS-based;
- JCR Q3 where possible;
- normal scholarly reputation;
- no mandatory pay-to-publish route;
- plausible scope fit for an analyst coverage, information environment, and future financial stress paper.

## Candidates Checked

### Economic Record

Decision: **Useful newly identified economics backup; not a replacement for APJFS.**

Evidence:

- The public JCR 2024 export lists `ECONOMIC RECORD` as `ECONOMICS`, `SSCI`, JIF 1.2, Q3.
- Wiley Journal Finder search-result snapshots report `Economic Record` with 16 days to first decision, a 37% acceptance rate, JIF 1.2, and an optional open-access APC.
- The journal has an Australia/economics identity, which is a better regional match than many generic European or US-only economics outlets.

Fit:

- The current manuscript would need a lighter economics/policy framing: analyst coverage as a market information channel in Australia and Singapore, with future financial stress as an applied firm-level outcome.
- It is less naturally finance-journal fit than APJFS, but more credible as a full-paper economics backup than a short-letter-only route.

Result:

- Add as a genuine transfer backup after APJFS if the user wants a regular SSCI Q3 economics ladder.
- Do not call it 70-80%. The best current visible acceptance-rate signal is 37%.

### Asian Economic Journal

Decision: **Regional economics backup only; not a high-probability route.**

Evidence:

- The public JCR 2024 export lists `Asian Economic Journal` as `ECONOMICS`, `SSCI`, JIF 1.1, Q3.
- Wiley Journal Finder search-result snapshots report a 22% acceptance rate.

Fit:

- Asia-focused economics scope is relevant, but the current manuscript is more corporate finance and capital-market information environment than a general Asian-economics paper.

Result:

- Possible later backup if the paper is reframed more heavily around Asia-Pacific applied economics.
- Not a 70-80% route.

### Pacific Economic Review

Decision: **Regional Asia-Pacific economics backup only; not a high-probability route.**

Evidence:

- Public JCR-style sources list `Pacific Economic Review` as SSCI/JCR Q3 economics.
- Wiley Journal Finder search-result snapshots report 119 days to first decision and a 22% acceptance rate.

Fit:

- The Asia-Pacific orientation is useful.
- The manuscript would need to move further from a finance-journal contribution toward applied economics and regional information environments.

Result:

- Keep only as a transfer-ladder name, not as APJFS replacement and not as a 70-80% route.

### Australian Economic Review

Decision: **Scope-adjacent Australia policy outlet; not enough probability evidence.**

Evidence:

- The public JCR 2024 export lists `Australian Economic Review` as `ECONOMICS`, `SSCI`, JIF 1.0, Q3.
- Public scope summaries describe it as applied economics with a strong policy orientation, particularly relevant to the Australian economy and broader Asia-Pacific region.

Fit:

- Australian-market relevance is good, but the manuscript uses both Singapore and Australia and is a firm-level analyst-coverage finance paper rather than a policy-economics paper.

Result:

- Not a current primary route. It could be considered only after a stronger applied-economics/policy remap.

### Economic Inquiry

Decision: **Reputable but too broad/ambitious for the stability route.**

Evidence:

- The public JCR 2024 export lists `ECONOMIC INQUIRY` as `ECONOMICS`, `SSCI`, JIF 1.3, Q3.
- WEAI describes it as a general-interest economics journal that has published work by many leading economists and uses submission fees of USD 100 for members and USD 150 for non-members.

Fit:

- It is a regular, reputable economics outlet, but the current paper is not broad enough for a general-interest economics journal without a substantial contribution reframing.

Result:

- Not a stability route and not a 70-80% route.

### The Quarterly Review of Economics and Finance

Decision: **Good scope, but not the requested Q3/70-80 route.**

Evidence:

- Elsevier lists QREF as covering economics, financial economics, and finance.
- Elsevier lists SSCI indexing and a subscription route with no publication fee charged to authors.
- Elsevier's current page shows JIF 3.1, which does not fit the requested Q3 stability route under current visible metrics.

Fit:

- The paper's finance and financial-stress content fits better here than in many broad economics journals.

Result:

- Keep only as a higher-tier or non-Q3 transfer idea, not as the user's strict target.

### Journal of Multinational Financial Management

Decision: **Good finance scope, but not the requested Q3/70-80 route.**

Evidence:

- Elsevier lists the journal as SSCI-indexed, with finance/economics subject areas and a subscription route with no publication fee charged to authors.
- Elsevier reports JIF 4.0 and CiteScore 7.0.

Fit:

- Some multinational finance scope is relevant, but the current paper is not primarily about multinational financial management.

Result:

- Not a Q3 stability route and not a 70-80% route.

## Round 15 Conclusion

Round 15 improves the transfer ladder but still does not solve the user's exact single-journal target.

The useful new addition is **Economic Record**: it is a regular SSCI/JCR Q3 economics journal with an official-publisher search-result acceptance signal of 37% and a plausible Australia/regional economics fit. It should be treated as a full-paper economics backup, not as a replacement for APJFS and not as a 70-80% journal.

The updated ladder under the strict reputation constraint is:

1. APJFS: best finance and Asia-Pacific capital-market fit.
2. Economic Record: useful full-paper economics backup after a lighter applied-economics remap.
3. APJAE: accounting/economics transfer candidate.
4. AEL / Applied Economics: probability-first transfer options, with AEL as compressed fallback and Applied Economics as broader non-Q3 fallback.

No Round 15 outlet supports a verified single-journal 70-80% acceptance claim.
