# Regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 Journal Screen - Round 11

Date: 2026-06-01

## Purpose

This round checked boundary cases in `BUSINESS, FINANCE` and nearby JCR categories. The question was whether a normal-reputation SSCI/JCR Q3 journal outside mainstream finance could offer a materially more stable route without falling below regular SSCI expectations or creating desk-rejection risk.

## Candidates Checked

### Journal of Pension Economics & Finance

Decision: **Exclude for this paper; not a general finance route.**

Evidence:

- Public JCR 2024 evidence lists `Journal of Pension Economics & Finance` in `BUSINESS, FINANCE`, SSCI, JIF Q3.
- Cambridge states that the journal focuses on the economics and finance of pensions and retirement income.
- Cambridge author instructions describe examples such as pensions and labour markets, pension fund management, pension regulation, pension liabilities, corporate governance, political economy, financial planning for retirement, and marketing of private pensions.
- No current official high acceptance-rate signal was found.

Fit:

- The paper's topic is analyst coverage and future financial stress in listed firms, not pensions, retirement income, or pension-fund finance.
- A forced submission would be desk-rejection risk despite the journal's strict SSCI/JCR Q3 signal.

Result:

- Not a route for the current paper and not evidence of a 70-80% stable acceptance outlet.

### International Journal of Auditing

Decision: **Exclude unless the paper is rebuilt as an auditing/accounting assurance paper.**

Evidence:

- Public JCR 2024 evidence lists `International Journal of Auditing` in `BUSINESS, FINANCE`, SSCI, JIF Q3.
- Scope evidence describes a journal focused on financial statement audits, internal auditing, audit quality, audit fees, audit regulation, independence, legal liability, sustainability audits, and audit assurance issues.
- No current official high acceptance-rate signal was found; older or third-party acceptance-rate snippets are not sufficient for the user's strict current route.

Fit:

- The current paper is about analyst coverage as a capital-market information-environment marker, not financial-statement audit quality or assurance.
- A submission here would require a different paper centered on audit or assurance mechanisms.

Result:

- Strict index/quartile evidence may exist, but scope fit fails and acceptance probability is unverified.

### International Journal of Health Economics and Management

Decision: **Exclude as wrong-field.**

Evidence:

- Public JCR 2024 evidence lists `International Journal of Health Economics and Management` in `BUSINESS, FINANCE`, SSCI, JIF Q3, and also in health/economics-related categories.
- JSTOR/Springer-facing information describes the focus as health care systems and the behavior of consumers, patients, and providers, with links among management, public policy, payment, and performance.
- No relevant high acceptance-rate signal was found.

Fit:

- The paper has no health-care, patient, provider, health-system, or health-policy component.

Result:

- Do not use as a technical JCR loophole. It is regular enough as a journal, but wrong for this manuscript.

### International Journal of Finance & Economics

Decision: **Possible broader finance/economics backup, but not a strict Q3/70-80 route.**

Evidence:

- Public JCR 2024 evidence lists `International Journal of Finance & Economics` in `BUSINESS, FINANCE`, SSCI, but the visible current JIF quartile signal is Q2 rather than Q3.
- Its scope is broad international finance and economics.
- No current official high acceptance-rate signal was found; non-official snippets around low acceptance are not sufficient to support a high-probability claim.

Fit:

- Topical fit is plausible, but the paper would need a stronger international-finance framing than the current APJFS version.

Result:

- Keep only as a higher-risk transfer backup, not as the requested strict Q3 70-80% outlet.

### International Review of Finance

Decision: **Possible higher-tier finance backup, not a probability route.**

Evidence:

- Public JCR 2024 evidence lists `International Review of Finance` in `BUSINESS, FINANCE`, SSCI, with JIF Q2 and AIS Q3-type evidence.
- Public ranking profiles place it in finance/economics rather than as a broad low-barrier outlet.

Fit:

- Better finance fit than pensions/auditing/health outlets, but likely more selective than APJFS and not Q3 by the visible JIF signal.

Result:

- Not a 70-80% route.

## Round 11 Conclusion

Round 11 confirms that many apparent `BUSINESS, FINANCE` Q3 opportunities are category-edge journals whose real editorial scope is pensions, auditing, health economics, or other specialized fields. Using them would not satisfy the user's reputation condition in substance because the manuscript would be out of scope.

No Round 11 outlet improves on APJFS for the current paper. The best route remains:

1. APJFS as the cleanest regular SSCI/JCR Q3 finance fit;
2. APJAE/AEL/Applied Economics as transfer/compression routes;
3. no single-journal 70-80% claim unless a scope-matched journal publishes credible current acceptance evidence.
