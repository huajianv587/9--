# Regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 Journal Screen

Date: 2026-06-01

## User Constraint

The target is not CAS/CAS-zone based. The target is:

- SCIE or SSCI Core Collection journal;
- JCR Q3, preferably in Business, Finance, Economics, Accounting, Risk, or a tightly related category;
- ordinary scholarly reputation, not obviously below a regular SSCI finance/economics journal expectation;
- scope fit for an empirical company-level panel paper on analyst coverage and future financial stress;
- paid submission fee or optional APC is acceptable, but "pay-to-publish" or low-reputation outlet is not acceptable.

## Hard Finding

I cannot honestly identify a regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 finance/economics journal with a credible single-journal 70-80% acceptance rate.

The journals that satisfy index/quartile/reputation/scope do not publish 70-80% acceptance signals. The outlets that appear easier are usually ESCI, Scopus-only, wrong-field, or reputation-risk journals. Therefore the 70-80% target can only be treated as a portfolio/ladder probability, not a single-journal promise.

## Best Regular Q3 Primary Candidate

### Asia-Pacific Journal of Financial Studies

Decision: **Primary remap candidate**

Evidence:

- JCR 2024 public export lists Asia-Pacific Journal of Financial Studies as BUSINESS, FINANCE, SSCI, JIF 1.5, Q3.
- The journal's author instructions describe it as the official English-language journal of the Korean Securities Association.
- Its scope covers empirical and theoretical capital-market research, investments, capital and security markets, corporate finance, and quantitative methods relevant to finance.
- The journal states double-blind peer review by two anonymous reviewers and the editor.
- The author guidelines state a submission fee of KRW 100,000 or USD 100 for members, KRW 150,000 or USD 150 for non-members, and no additional publication fee.
- The association page states the journal has been selected for SSCI coverage as of 2006 and describes it as an SSCI-included finance journal in Asia.

Fit:

- Strongest fit for the current paper because the paper uses Singapore and Australian listed-firm data, analyst coverage, capital-market information environment, and future financial stress.
- Better reputation fit than low-barrier OA or ESCI-only alternatives.

Probability:

- Cannot be called 70-80% at the single-journal level.
- With a full APJFS-style manuscript, the realistic single-journal probability is materially below 70%, but likely stronger than a poorly scoped nominal Q3 outlet.

Required remap:

- Expand from short AEL letter to a full finance paper.
- Add current-stress robustness and financial-stress onset checks.
- Move prediction performance to secondary evidence.
- Emphasize analyst coverage as an information-environment marker in thinly covered Asia-Pacific markets.
- Use double-blind main manuscript and separate title page.

## Secondary Regular Q3 Candidate

### Asia-Pacific Journal of Accounting & Economics

Decision: **Secondary transfer candidate**

Evidence:

- JCR 2024 public export lists Asia-Pacific Journal of Accounting & Economics as ECONOMICS, SSCI, Q2 and BUSINESS, FINANCE, SSCI, Q3.
- Taylor & Francis' journal page describes APJAE as an international forum for analytical and empirical archival and experimental research in industrial organization, financial and product markets, and accounting information.
- The same page states that it publishes theoretical and empirical research in economics and accounting, including financial analysts.
- The current Taylor & Francis journal-metrics page reports a 23% acceptance rate, which is useful as a transfer signal but still far below 70-80%.

Fit:

- Good topical fit if the paper is framed through financial analysts, accounting information, and firm financial stress.
- Weaker than APJFS as the primary target because the current contribution is more capital-market/finance than accounting.

Probability:

- Not 70-80% single-journal. The current official acceptance-rate signal is 23%.

## Existing AEL Route

### Applied Economics Letters

Decision: **Tertiary fast-letter fallback, not final target**

Evidence:

- T&F journal information publicly displays a 35% acceptance rate and SSCI indexing.
- Public JCR/Peeref/LetPub records show current Economics Q3 signals.

Fit:

- Good for a short applied-economics letter.
- Lower reputation fit than APJFS for a full finance paper, and the official acceptance rate is far below 70-80%.

## Exclusions

- JAFEB-style route: exclude unless current Web of Science/JCR status and reputation are re-verified; this does not meet the user's "regular SSCI-level reputation" constraint.
- ESCI-only journals: exclude.
- Scopus-only Q3 journals: exclude.
- MDPI finance/economics candidates such as IJFS/JRFM: do not use as primary under the "reputation not obviously below regular SSCI expectations" constraint unless the user explicitly relaxes that condition.
- Wrong-field SCIE high-acceptance journals: exclude even if Q3 and technically SCIE, because scope mismatch raises desk-rejection and reputation risk.
- Eastern European Economics: exclude for this paper because the geographic scope is poor.

## Working Ladder Under the User's Constraint

1. **Primary:** Asia-Pacific Journal of Financial Studies.
2. **Transfer 1:** Economic Record, if APJFS rejects without a fatal data objection and the paper is reframed toward Australia/Asia-Pacific applied economics.
3. **Transfer 2:** Applied Economics, only if Q2-or-better is acceptable and the paper is expanded as a fuller applied-economics article.
4. **Compressed fallback:** Applied Economics Letters.
5. **Re-check only if needed:** Asia-Pacific Journal of Accounting & Economics remains a topical accounting/economics name, but it is not the current packaged transfer route and its visible acceptance signal is only 23%.

This ladder is the most defensible way to approach a 70-80% project-level probability while preserving regular SCIE/SSCI reputation. It does not create a true 70-80% single-journal guarantee.
