# Regular SCIE/SSCI Q3 Journal Screen, Round 3

Date: 2026-06-01

## Purpose

This round specifically tests whether the user's exact target can be met by moving beyond finance-only outlets: regular SCIE/SSCI-level journal, JCR Q3 signal, normal scholarly reputation, not CAS-based, and a credible 70-80% single-journal acceptance signal.

## New Finding

The only materially new candidate worth keeping under observation is **Journal of Business Economics and Management**. It is not a confirmed 70-80% route, but it is a regular SSCI/JCR journal with a Q3 Business category and broader business/economics scope that includes finance and investment.

## Interpretation

Round 3 still does **not** prove a reputable regular SSCI/Q3 single-journal 70-80% route for the current finance paper.

The practical update is:

1. **APJFS remains the best reputation-fit finance primary target.**
2. **Journal of Business Economics and Management is now the best probability-seeking exploratory backup**, because it is regular SSCI/JCR, has a Q3 Business category, and its scope includes finance and investment.
3. The claimed near-80% acceptance for JBEM is not official and must not be represented as verified.
4. If the user prioritizes probability over finance-society fit, the next manuscript route should be a JBEM transfer draft with a broader business/economics framing.
